Ratan Tata

Businessman • Philanthropist • Former Chairman of Tata Sons


"The milestones in Ratan Tata's remarkable journey."

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist and a former chairman of Tata Sons. From 1990 – 2012 he was the Chairman of Tata Group and became the interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He is a dedicated philanthropist and heads the company’s charitable trust, and more than half of the profits are channelled towards various charitable initiatives. He was awarded the second and the third highest civilian award in India – the Padma Bhushan – in 2000 and the Padma Vibhushan – in 2008.

Timeline of Ratan Tata

Early Life and Education
  • 28 December 1937: Ratan Naval Tata is born in Mumbai, India.
  • 1955: Ratan Tata completes his graduation from Cornell University in New York.
  • 1959: He receives a degree in architecture from Cornell University.
Professional Career
  • 1961: Ratan Tata joins Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group.
  • 1971: He becomes the director-in-charge of the National Radio & Electronics Company Limited (NELCO).
  • 1991: Ratan Tata becomes the chairman of Tata Industries.
  • 1991-2012: He serves as the chairman of Tata Sons, leading the Tata Group to new heights.
  • 1998: Ratan Tata receives the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.
  • 2000: He is awarded the honorary title of Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire.
Philanthropy and Social Initiatives
  • 1996: Ratan Tata initiates the Tata Tea Jaago Re campaign, focusing on social awakening.
  • 2008: He launches the Tata Nano, an affordable car designed for the masses.
  • 2010: Ratan Tata donates $50 million to establish the Tata Medical Center in Kolkata.
  • 2014: He pledges to donate the majority of his wealth to philanthropic causes.
  • 2020: Ratan Tata actively contributes to COVID-19 relief efforts in India.
